s. 140(2) Land Titles Act — The Foundational Principle:
The Land Titles system guarantees the QUALITY of title, not the EXTENT of land.
This is the single most important principle in the course. Cite it whenever title quality or indefeasibility is at issue.

How to Use This on Exam Day

Step 1: Read the entire fact pattern — identify the stage of the transaction (pre-APS, APS, title search, closing, post-closing)
Step 2: Open the Exam Checklist tab and tick every applicable issue
Step 3: For each issue, navigate to the relevant Pillar and open the FIRAC template
Step 4: Always identify which title system applies (Registry or Land Titles) — the answer may differ
Step 5: For remedies → default to specific performance (land is unique); distinguish from damages only if facts demand it
